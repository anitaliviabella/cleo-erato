About

Cleo & Erato is a magazine, so called in honor of the names of the two Greek muses.
In ancient Greek religion and mythology, the Muses are the inspirational goddesses of literature, science, and the arts.
They were considered the source of the knowledge embodied in the poetry, lyric songs, and myths.
We've choosen Cleo, the muse of history, and Erato, the muse of lyric poetry, in order to represent our central theme:
Women's poetry across history and across the centuries.
Respectively, Erato represents poetry and Cleo represents history.
Then, we've choosen three issues in order to represent poetry, in a chronological order, through centuries:
Those are the Ancient Era, the XIX century and the XXI century.
We've choosen a poetess for each historical period and those are, respectively:
Sappho, for what concerns the Ancient Era.
Emily Dickinson, who represents the XIX century.
Rupi Kaur, for the XXI century.
For each poetess we've choosen n significative poems (link).
